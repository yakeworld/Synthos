I am pleased to submit our manuscript entitled "Synthos: A Self-Evolving Cognitive Operating System for AI-Augmented Scientific Research" for consideration for publication in Intelligent Computing.

**Summary**

Large language model (LLM) agents have demonstrated remarkable potential in scientific research assistance, yet existing systems remain fragmented between tool orchestration and genuine cognitive reasoning. Most architectures embed cognitive workflows within Python orchestration layers, creating tight coupling between reasoning processes and execution infrastructure. No existing system simultaneously achieves explicit epistemological encoding of scientific principles, constitutional governance of agent behavior, self-evolving architecture, and zero-Python execution.

We present Synthos, a cognitive operating system that addresses all four gaps simultaneously through three innovations:

1. **Epistemological Code**: Eight philosophical frameworks from the history and philosophy of science (Falsificationism, Bayesian Reasoning, Occam's Razor, Systems Thinking, etc.) are reified as executable SKILL.md cognitive atoms with formal I/O contracts and provably non-overlapping boundaries.

2. **Constitutional Hierarchy**: All system behavior is governed by a formal hierarchy of immutable principles (CON > MEM > CMD > SKL > DEF), enforced by a philosophical immune system with drift detection.

3. **Self-Evolution Infrastructure**: An 11-step evolution engine (v2.11) incorporating SEPL rollback, Git-as-Memory, hypothesis-first single-metric improvement, and active inference gates operationalizing the Free Energy Principle.

Over 41 autonomous evolution cycles, Synthos achieved a composite quality score of 0.975 (EXCELLENT), maintained all cognitive atoms at perfect structural and benchmark scores of 1.00, absorbed patterns from 12 external open-source projects, and produced paper drafts across four biomedical domains. The system executes with zero Python infrastructure code — the Agent itself serves as the runtime.

**Why Intelligent Computing?**

Synthos directly addresses the journal's scope at the intersection of novel computing architectures and scientific discovery. The system's architectural innovations — particularly its demonstration that epistemological principles can be operationalized as executable computational constraints — represent a fundamental advance in how AI agents can be designed, governed, and evolved for scientific research.
