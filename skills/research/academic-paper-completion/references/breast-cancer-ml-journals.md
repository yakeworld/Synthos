# Target Journals for Breast Cancer ML Papers

Curated analysis from May 2026 journal selection session for HCS-3WT breast cancer diagnosis paper.

## TIER 1 — Strong Match (Target First)

### Artificial Intelligence in Medicine (Elsevier, IF: 7.0, Q1)
- **Fit**: 10/10 — Perfect match for medical AI diagnosis
- **Focus**: AI in healthcare, clinical decision support, medical diagnosis
- **Strengths**: Highly active in clinical AI papers; values methodology rigor and clinical interpretability
- **Notes**: Emphasize clinical significance and explainability in submission

### Computers in Biology and Medicine (Elsevier, IF: 7.8, Q1)
- **Fit**: 9/10 — Excellent fit for breast cancer ML
- **Focus**: Bioinformatics, computational biology, medical imaging, ML in healthcare
- **Strengths**: Core publication venue for breast cancer ML papers
- **Notes**: Acknowledge limitations in Discussion section

### IEEE Journal of Biomedical and Health Informatics (IEEE, IF: 7.7, Q1)
- **Fit**: 9/10 — Strong technical match
- **Focus**: Biomedical informatics, healthcare AI, signal processing
- **Strengths**: IEEE flagship in biomedical informatics; values rigorous methodology
- **Notes**: Requires more detailed algorithm analysis and complexity discussion

## TIER 2 — Good Match (Backup)

### Computer Methods and Programs in Biomedicine (Elsevier, IF: 6.1, Q1)
- **Fit**: 8/10 — Good for methodological papers
- **Focus**: Computational methods, medical software, clinical AI
- **Notes**: Emphasize clinical application scenario

### Expert Systems with Applications (Elsevier, IF: 8.5, Q1)
- **Fit**: 7/10 — High IF but CS-focused
- **Focus**: Expert systems, ML, decision support
- **Notes**: Frame as "expert system" and "decision support" narrative

### npj Digital Medicine (Springer Nature, IF: 9.5, Q1)
- **Fit**: 7/10 — Very high IF but clinical/trial focused
- **Focus**: Digital health, AI in medicine, clinical trials, mHealth
- **Notes**: May find WDBC dataset insufficient for this journal's standards

## TIER 3 — Acceptable (If Rejected from Higher Tiers)

### BMC Medical Informatics and Decision Making (Springer Nature, IF: 2.9, Q2, OA)
- **Fit**: 6/10 — Good for methodology, open access
- **Notes**: Faster review, but lower impact

### Scientific Reports (Nature Portfolio, IF: 3.8, Q2, OA)
- **Fit**: 5/10 — Broad scope, lower prestige
- **Notes**: Fallback option

### PLOS ONE (PLOS, IF: 3.7, Q2, OA)
- **Fit**: 4/10 — Very broad, lower prestige
- **Notes**: Last resort only

## Selection Methodology

1. Search PubMed/OpenAlex for similar published papers in target journals
2. Verify recent issues show acceptance of similar work
3. Compare paper quality to journal's typical output
4. Factor in IF, Q1/Q2, OA status, APC costs
5. Prepare supplementary material preemptively
6. Have fallback journal identified before first submission
